The data of my project and the visualizations I plan to use

The data I will be using is primiarly from this github page. The data is in .rda format and I will using RStudio to convert them into .csv files as I am not experienced in handling R files. This is done through the use of write.csv() command in RStudio that I learnt through this video.
Additional data, if needed, would be retrived and keyed in manually from the Survivor Wiki Page.

To start the data story off, I would be attempting to use animation to show the general flow of the gameplay through Plotly Animations

The data for where the show has been filmed is not in the R data so I will be using the Survivor wiki for the data. In terms of visualisations, I will be using plotly choropleth maps to show both the locations of where the show has filmed (World Map) and where the contestants are from (US's map).

In terms of the viewership and rating of the show, I will primiarly be using viewers.rda for my data source (which is then converted to viewers.csv). For visualisations of data for the viewership and rating, I will first make 2 line graphs showcasing the change in viewership with the change in show's rating over the period of the entire run of the show.

Then, the trends of the shows will be similarly be shown through simple bar and line charts, possibly through plotly.

Afterwards, episodes and seasons with the highest ratings will be highlighted as a way to guide the new viewer to choose which season to watch first. This is done through the use of barcharts. I plan to add custom events to each barchart that will give a synopsis of the episode / season but I am struggling to find a method for it through chart.js so I might shift this over to plotly if need be.

I also want to highlight Season 40 of the show : Winners at War, as it is the season with all winners of previous seasons playing so I want to use an interactive method to showcase the relationship between those winners and the seasons they have played together in. For the visualisation of this component, I found a method called force directed graph that I feel would be suitable for this.

5 Questions

What is your project about?
US version of Survivor Reality TV competition

What is the data you plan to use?
The show's ratings / viewership, demographics LINK

What is the question you plan to answer?
Why should I watch the show and where should I start watching

Why is this an important question?
Survivor is one of the longest running reality TV show that have lasted for 23 years (and counting).
Survivor's ratings have always been high on average but the viewership is on a decline, so it is important to introduce new viewers to the show.
Survivor does a good job at representation through a diverse cast so it carries a lot of important stories and narrativies that need to be seen.

Which rows and columns of the dataset do you plan to use, to answer this question?
I am using multiple data files but for viewers.csv, I am using labels of Seasons, Average imdb_rating and viewers number. Since the data includes Australian and South African version of the show, I will be filtering those data out.
In terms of the castaways.csv, I will be using the order of their elimination and then will be filtering out the Australian and South African version of the show.
